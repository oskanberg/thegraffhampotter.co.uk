Profile

I developed a love of pottery more than 10 years ago when I wanted to make bonsai pots for my miniature trees. I signed up to an art class, and from the very first tiny coil pot I made I was in love with clay.

The silken feel of soft clay in my fingers is cooling calming and absorbing. It transports me both back in time to the mud pies of my childhood, and also into a meditative state where marvellous creations are possible and time has no meaning.

I have studied with some talented potters including, Carolyn Genders, Alison Sandeman and Shozo Michikawa.

The Studio

Newly extended, my studio has space for up to six people, so come and try your hand at pottery. I can offer tuition in hand building techniques such as coiling, slab building and moulded forms. I also offer throwing on the potters wheel and have some knowledge of sculpting animal forms.

Your Pottery Experience

About

In order to get the best out of your time in my studio you should plan to come at least three times, in order to be able to build or throw your piece, then refine and finish it when it has dried to leather hard. Finally the fun of decorating and glazing you piece before its final firing completes your sessions. This will enable you to make a small piece, larger pieces take more time

Cost

Each Session is three hours from 9-12am. The cost is £36 per session or if you prefer £12 per hour.

There is an addition small charge of £5 per kilo of finished work, to cover materials and firing. This allows you to choose the size of your creation, be it a tiny egg cup or a large statue.
